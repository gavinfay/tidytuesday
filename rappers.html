1 TidyTuesday16

For this weeks #TIdyTuesday I wanted to try out scrollytelling, with @yihuis rolldown package.

I investigated the best rap artists dataset, containing ranks from BBC Music on critics favorite hip-hop tracks.

Data & TidyTuesday:
https://github.com/rfordatascience/tidytuesday/blob/master/data/2020/2020-04-14/readme.md

100+ critics ranked their favorite tracks, and then these ranks were aggregated to give each track an overall point score based on how many times they received rankings.

1.1 When were the best hip-hop tracks released?

Summarizing these scores over time, we see an overall decline over time in the score of tracks. Fitting a Poisson GLM to the scores for ranked tracks (the scoring of ranks method wont result in Poisson but for our purposes this may be sufficient), we find the average points score for a track decreased by 0% per year. The predicted average score for a ranked track in 1979 was 22, and this predicted average score in 2019 was 5.

3 More women represented over time?

We see that the chance that the highest scoring track in a year was by a female artist almost doubles over the time series, from 9% to 16%.

4 Positivity

Applying sentiment analysis to the words in the titles of tracks, we see that 90s track titles contained more positive words than tracks receiving votes released in other decades.
Sentiments of positivity and joy were relatively more frequent in the 90s, as were words of SURPRISE!
